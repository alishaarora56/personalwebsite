Alisha Arora

I develop Machine Learning systems and software tools to disrupt industries and solve global problems in healthcare, climate, and beyond. I am a Systems Design Engineering student at the University of Waterloo, Canada's top engineering school. Currently, I’m at TELUS optimizing LLMs for energy efficiency and developing a full-stack carbon capture approximation platform.

Previously, I conducted research under Dr. Ghosh at MIT, developing machine learning models to explore the relationship between the gut microbiome and schizophrenia. At Procter & Gamble, I worked on the Always YOU app, building the back-end for a menstrual and well-being tracker created in collaboration with Thrive Global. I also worked on a chatbot for Vitala Global’s abortion care tool, enhancing accessibility for users worldwide.

Before this, I co-founded The HopeSisters, a non-profit dedicated to supporting children in foster care and spreading hope to vulnerable communities. During the pandemic, we delivered 5,000+ HopeBags to children in care and 12,000+ handwritten cards to seniors, spreading kindness and connection during a time of isolation.

My passion for combining technology and social impact has led me to be recognized as one of Canada’s Top 100 Most Powerful Women, serve as a member of the World Economic Forum’s AI Council, and as a UNICEF Youth Ambassador. My work has been featured on Forbes, Microsoft, and I’ve spoken to over 30,000 people at various tech conferences.
